Project Title: Parallel Metropolis-Hastings algorithms

Name: Boyan Bejanov

Project Outline:

Metropolis-Hastings algorithm is a Markov chain algorithm for simulating random samples from multi-dimensional probability distributions for which direct sampling is difficult. Standard applications include the Markov Chain Monte Carlo method, Bayesian inference and others.

The parallelization of the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm is a hot topic of both theoretical research and practical implementations. The main difficulty is in the fact that Markov chains are, by definition, sequential in nature, because state n+1 depends on state n. In addition, the Markov chain's burn-in times become very long in high-dimensional problems, which renders the option of simulating multiple independent Markov chains impractical, because each chain must go through its own burn-in.

The objectives of this project include

  • Survey the currently used methods and critically evaluate them.
  • Identify the most promising method and implement it.
  • Investigate the benefits of parallelization in terms of theoretical and actually achieved speedups, applicability, other relevant criteria.
